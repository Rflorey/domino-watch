The quant anchors paint a fragile carry picture. Yen-denominated crude spiked ¥868 to ¥15,568/bbl, a direct hit to Japan's terms of trade and a boost to imported inflation—exactly the dynamic that forces the BOJ's hand. The BOJ meets April 30; market consensus is hold at 0.75%, but reports indicate policymakers will signal readiness to resume normalization as early as June if energy-driven inflation persists. USDJPY's 5-day range (157.86–159.82) shows it's coiled at the top; a break above 160 likely draws intervention, a rejection could ease near-term pressure. Domino sequence: we're at Stage 2 (spread wide, but BOJ rhetoric shifting + intervention risk rising). Not yet Stage 3 (spread compressing, yen strengthening, unwinding).

Crypto liquidity read is mixed. BTC stalled at $78,126, failing to reclaim $80k despite a 5-day rally; 30-day correlation with DXY hit -0.90 (most negative since 2022), and open interest dropped 6% in 24 hours—levered longs are unwinding, suggesting risk appetite is cooling even as equities rally. XRP gained 1.2% to $1.43, benefiting from post-SEC clarity but volume is tepid. The yen carry unwind thesis suggests that if USDJPY reverses sharply, yen-funded leverage across risk assets (including crypto) unwinds in tandem. Right now, BTC's failure to break out + rising DXY inverse correlation + falling open interest = early-stage risk-off positioning. Russ's 15,300 XRP is up modestly month-over-month but faces headwinds if broader carry unwind accelerates.
