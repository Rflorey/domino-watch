The scenario distribution is balanced: 60% base case (25bp hike, orderly USDJPY retracement to 158-160, grade moves to D by week 4), 25% dovish tail (USDJPY spikes, intervention, grade moves to B), 15% hawkish tail (yen rallies, carry unwinds, grade moves to B).   • Wk 3 → Wk 4: Week 4 holds C (Watchful) but with *applied unwind prior as tiebreaker*—otherwise would be graded D (Unlikely). The rationale: by week 4 (post-BOJ July 31), the median scenario is constructive for carry stability—a 25bp hike to 1.25%, USDJPY retracement to 158-160 range, spread compression to ~160 bps (still wide enough to sustain carry but less attractive), and volatility mean-reversion as intervention risk dissipates. In that base case, unwind risk declines sharply and the grade would drop to D by mid-August. However, two tail risks justify holding C in week 4: (1) if the BOJ disappoints dovishly (holds at 1% or signals slow pace), USDJPY could remain elevated above 162 into early August, keeping MOF intervention risk live and the regime fragile; (2) if Middle East tensions re-escalate (Iran peace talks collapse, Hormuz blockade reinstated), oil could spike back above $90, reversing the disinflationary tailwind and forcing the BOJ into emergency hawkishness while the Fed pauses, compressing the spread rapidly. The unwind prior leans one notch more alert (D→C) given that USDJPY is currently 11 pips from the intervention line—this proximity creates path-dependence where a small shock (e.g., strong US CPI print, geopolitical flare) could cascade quickly into Stage 3. If the BOJ meeting passes uneventfully and USDJPY retraces below 160 by August 7, the 4-week-forward grade (for August 14) would drop to D. Week 3 to week 4 both remain C, but week 4 is the inflection point where the grade either drops to D (base case) or elevates to B (tail scenarios).
