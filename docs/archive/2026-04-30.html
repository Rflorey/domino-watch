The Narrative

Overnight the carry trade thesis moved from Stage 1 (Pressure Building) to Stage 3 (Shock/Trigger) as USDJPY briefly pierced 160.37 before collapsing 2.38% to 156.57 by NY morning. The catalyst: BOJ's April-28 hold at 0.75% was accompanied by three of nine board members voting for an immediate hike—the highest dissent since 1992—and a risk scenario forecasting core inflation at 3.1% in FY2026 if oil stays above $105. WTI crude spiked to $111 intraday Wednesday before settling at $107, then pulled back to $105 today as Trump rejected Iran's latest Hormuz proposal. JGB 10Y yield surged 6.5 bps to 2.53%, a 25-year high. Fed held rates as expected but also saw three dissenters opposing the easing bias, pushing US 10Y to 4.42%. The combo—BOJ hawkish pivot + sustained energy shock + 160 breach—triggered acute unwind. Regime upgraded from yellow to red. Stage moved from 1 to 3.

The quant anchors confirm the shift. US-JP spread compressed from ~200 bps Tuesday to 189 bps today (US 10Y 4.42% minus JGB 2.53%). USDJPY 1M implied vol spiked from ~7% to ~8.5%, collapsing the carry-to-vol ratio from 27 to 22—still positive but eroding fast. USDJPY's 5-day move is -2.4%, well above MOF's historical pace threshold for verbal intervention. Distance from 60d mean: USDJPY was 159.82 Monday, now 156.57—likely stretched to the downside short-term, but the 60d mean itself has been drifting higher (~155), so we're near fair value post-unwind. The key: pace and volatility, not absolute level. 1-week grade B (Likely) reflects active unwind signatures—sharp FX move, vol spike, three BOJ dissenters, oil $105+. 2-week grade C (Watchful) because the unwind may pause: next BOJ meeting is June, spread is still wide, and MOVE/VIX are not signaling systemic cascade yet. Applied the unwind prior as a tiebreaker for 2w—data is mixed (unwind in FX, but equities/credit still orderly), so lean one notch more alert: would otherwise grade D, upgraded to C. 3-week and 4-week grades C and D respectively, weighted by scenario: if oil retreats and Iran deal progresses, acute risk fades; if oil sustains >$110, we're in Stage 4 forced liquidation.

BTC at $76,095 is down only 0.2% despite the FX vol spike and equity selloff in Japan. XRP at ~$1.37-$1.39 range, flat to slightly down. This decoupling is notable: crypto should drain in a classic risk-off unwind, yet BTC has held $75k-$77k for weeks. Two reads: (1) Crypto liquidity is structurally independent of yen carry now—different marginal buyer (US institutions, ETFs). (2) Lag effect—if HY credit widens or equities crack further, BTC follows with a 1-3 day delay. For positioning: treat BTC stability as a yellow flag, not green. If Nikkei breaks below 58k or HY spreads widen >50bps in a week, expect crypto to give back 5-10%. If BTC breaks below $72k, that's confirmation the unwind is broadening beyond FX into levered risk assets. For now, crypto is a bystander, not a participant—but that can flip fast.
