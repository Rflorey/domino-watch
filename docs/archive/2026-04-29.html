The Narrative

The BOJ held rates at 0.75% in a 6-3 vote—the widest split under Ueda—with three members (Nakagawa, Takata, Tamura) demanding an immediate hike to 1.0% on upside inflation risk. The decision was a hawkish hold on paper, but Ueda's press conference delivered no clear signal on June timing, leaving USDJPY vulnerable. The yen briefly strengthened to 159 on the decision, then reversed to 159.82 by NY close (+0.12% on the day) as markets concluded verbal tightening without a roadmap is insufficient. Hours before the decision, Finance Minister Katayama issued her strongest intervention warning yet: 'ready to respond 24 hours a day, bold action when needed.' USDJPY now sits just 18 pips (0.18 yen) below the 160 line that triggered intervention in 2024. Oil held near $100 for a seventh session as Iran-US peace talks collapsed—Trump called the nuclear proposal inadequate. Nikkei sold off 1.3% on growth-cut fears (BOJ slashed FY26 GDP to 0.5% from 1.0%). No stage change from yesterday's yellow; we remain Stage 2 (Pre-Intervention) with elevated MOF rhetoric and USDJPY at the watch line.
