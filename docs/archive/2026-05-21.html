Horizon Rationale

  • Wk 1 → Wk 2: Week 1-2 grades both C because the BOJ June 15-16 meeting sits squarely in this window and is the highest-probability catalyst. If the BOJ hikes (50% market-implied odds) or if USDJPY crosses 160 before the meeting, we jump to Stage 3 (Shock/Trigger). If the meeting passes dovish and USDJPY stays sub-160, grades drop to D by week 3. The oil retreat (WTI sub-$100) is a meaningful relief valve—it removes one BOJ hawkish input (import inflation)—but JGB yields are already at 30-year highs and that genie is harder to re-bottle. Week 1-2 risk is elevated but not imminent; the trade is to watch USDJPY pace and any MOF rate-check rumors (which would precede intervention by hours/days).
  • Wk 2 → Wk 3: If the BOJ meeting on June 15-16 passes without a hike and USDJPY holds below 160, the setup cools materially. Grade drops from C to D because the near-term catalyst passes and the market gets clarity. The oil retreat supports this path—if Iran talks progress and WTI stays sub-$100, the BOJ loses urgency to hike in June and can wait until July or later. Conversely, if BOJ hikes or USDJPY breaks 160, week 3 becomes Stage 4 (Forced Unwind) and grades jump to B. This is a binary outcome hinging on one meeting.
  • Wk 3 → Wk 4: By week 4, assuming no June shock, the setup is D (Unlikely). Sufficient time has passed for policy clarity: either the BOJ hiked and the market digested it, or they held and USDJPY pressure diffused. The oil variable is key—if WTI stays sub-$100 into mid-June, the BOJ can afford to wait, and carry can stabilize. The risk is exogenous: if Iran talks collapse and oil spikes back above $110, or if a surprise Fed hike/China data shock triggers cross-asset selling, all bets are off. But absent that, the base case by week 4 is that we're back in Stage 1 (Pressure Building) rather than Stage 2 (Pre-Intervention). Grades reflect scenarios, not predictions: the modal outcome is that June passes without major disruption, but the tail risk (BOJ hike + MOF intervention) is non-trivial at 30-40%.
